Chicken Tikka Masala

This is my go to recipe for chicken tikka masala. It produces a creamy and delicious sauce with the correct amount of flavor. It is also versatile in that you can adjust the recipe to match your desired level of spicyness. There is a marinade phase to this recipe, so definitely account for that. I recommend a 6 hour marinade for the chicken.

Ingredients list:

  • 6 Garlic Cloves, minced (a garlic press is fine)
  • 4 tsp. Finely Grated Ginger (leave this out if you don't like ginger)
  • 4 tsp. Ground Turmeric
  • 2 tsp. Garam Masala
  • 2 tsp. Ground Coriander
  • 2 tsp. Ground Cumin
  • 1&1/2 Cups Whole-Milk Yogurt (this can be tough to find but it is buried with the rest of the yogurts I promise)
  • 1 tbsp. Kosher Salt
  • 2 lbs. boneless skinless chicken thighs.

    • Alternatively you can use boneless skinless chicken breasts but I prefer the thighs for this recipe.

  • 3 tbsp. Ghee
  • 1/4 Cup Tomato Paste
  • 6 Cardamom Pods or alternatively ground cardamom
  • 1/2 tsp. Crushed Red Pepper Flakes

    • This is where the spice adjustment comes in. You can up the amount of flakes or if you really want to you can substitute in dried arbol chiles. When making you selection for how many you want. 2 will give a decent relatively weak spice but still have something. 4 will get you some good spice and if you feel like, just keep upping it from there. It's your life after all, do what you want.

  • 1 28-oz. Can Whole Peeled Tomatoes, try to use San Marzano
  • 3/4 Cups Chopped Cilantro
  • Basmati Rice (however much you think you'll need)

    Preparation Phase:

  1. Prepare you chicken thighs but removing the nastier gristley pieces.
  2. We will need an oven on broil during the simmer phase of the sauce, so plan accordingly, I recommend reading everything before cooking.
  3. Preparation: Combine your garlic, turmeric, garam masala, coriander, cumin, and cardamom (if you are using ground cardamom since you couldn't find the pods). Combine the yogurt, salt, and half of that spice mixture you just whipped up and coat your chicken.
  4. Toss that chicken in the refrigerator and let it marinade for 4-6 hours
  5. Open up your tomatoe paste and your whole peeled tomatoes.

    6. Cooking Phase:

  6. Heat your Ghee in a suitably large pot. Manage your heat for this part. Add the onion, tomatoe paste, and cardamom pods (if you mananged to find them). Stir often and once the tomato paste shows even an inkling of starting to darken add the remaining half of the spice mixture. Keep stirring and as soon as you see a hint of browning on the bottom of the pot, add the whole peeled tomatoes by crushing each one with your hand into the pot.
  7. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer and stir often. Let simmer for about 8-10 minutes until you start to see things thicken up.
  8. Add the cream and the cilantro, mix things up and then let the whole mixture thicken up. Might take anywhere from 30-40 minutes depending on your heat management.

    9. While your sauce cooks:

  9. Preheat broiler if you haven't already done so. Line a baking sheet and toss your marinaded chicken thighs onto the pan. If you have a wire rack, add that to the sheet as well. The marinade sort of disintegrates into a liquid while under the broiler. It isn't mandatory to use the rack, I have done it both ways. The goal here is to broil until blackened spots begin to appear on the chicken (looking for that flavor).
  10. Once those blackened spots appear, pull the chicken and cut everything into bite size pieces. The chicken thighs will still be raw, they finish cooking in the sauce.

    11. What is missing from this recipe is the seriously charred aspect that is achieved with a tandoor oven. The broiler method won't really get us where we want to be but it is convenient. If you would like to try and replicate the tandoor oven as best you can then I would go for a charcoal grill brought to the hottest point you can manage and then char the chicken that way. The chicken should still be raw and then just proceed wih finishing the chicken in the sauce as noted.

  11. Serve everything up with rice and enjoy!
